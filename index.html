Introduction

YouTube is a popular platform for tech reviews and content that can significantly influence consumer decisions. In this ADA project, we have selected five of the top smartphone companies in terms of market share in recent years: Apple, Samsung, Huawei, Xiaomi, and Oppo. These companies receive "free marketing" through the coverage they receive in YouTube videos, which can include reviews and comparisons of smartphone products, particularly when they release new models.

Apple is the Most Valuable Brand in 2022 and also a highly talked-about company in traditional media and is also the largest and most influential of these smartphone companies. However, Apple releases fewer products compared to its competitors. Let’s investigate whether there is a systematic trend in the coverage and viewership of news related to Apple's "less is more" product release strategy, and how it compares in terms of video content production by YouTubers.

How did we get search Videos about each brand?

In order to go further in the analysis, we needed to be able to detect which videos talk about which brand. To do this, we built a list of keywords associated with each brand and then checked for each video if it contained one of the keywords associated with a given brand in either the title, the description or the tags. If it did, then the brand was added to the list of brands associated with this video. The tricky part was the generation of these keywords : if they are too generic, they will match even unrelated videos and if they are too specific, they will miss a lot of videos.

The way we did it is that we used the Phone to smartphone dataset to collect all the models released by a brand after 2005. Then we processed these models' names in order to make them simpler because most of the time the full name is not used when talking about a smartphone on the internet. For example a lot of videos will talk about the “new Iphone” and not explicitly the “Iphone XS MAX”. This methodology allowed us to retrieve enough videos for each brand while still making sure that the videos retrieved were relevant.

How do they differ in sentiment?

Although we have observed that Apple stands out from the other brands for this {}, we have obtained surprising results in the sentiment analysis so now we are going to do a sentimental analysis on the titles and on the descriptions of the videos for each brand. Note that we did not consider the tags in the analysis since they usually do not convey any significant sentiment since they are not meant to be read by the watcher of the video, indeed the titles and descriptions are the most important part of the video since they are the first thing that the watcher sees. In detail for each brand we compute the compound sentimental score of the title and of the description for each video, and we consider the average of these two scores as the final score of the video. We then plot a histogram of the scores for each brand.

Notice that the scores are in the range [-1,1] where -1 is the most negative score and 1 is the most positive score. Moreover it is interesting to notice that the scores are not normally distributed, but rather they are skewed to the right, which means that most of the videos have a positive score. It is surprising to notice that all the brands have a similar distribution of the scores, which means that the videos of each brand are similar in terms of sentiment. Indeed from the graph we can observe each brand as a spike of videos that have a score close to 0.5, which means that the videos of each brand are mostly positive. There is also another spike of videos that have a score exactly equal to 0, which means that the videos of each brand are mostly neutral, this is not surprising since we can expect some videos that are mostly about the products of the brand and they are not meant to be funny or to convey any other sentiment, but they are rather more to convey specifical technical information.

More Detail

To understand the following plot we need first understand how a time series can be decomposed: generally a time series consists of three systematic components: Level, Trend and Seasonality and one non-systematic component: the Noise. These components are defined as follows: level representing the average value in the series, trend representing the increasing or decreasing value in the series, seasonality representing the repeating short-term cycle in the series (e.g. weekly, yearly) and noise representing the random variation in the series. To represent a time series using these elements we can combine them in two different ways:

  • Additive Model: \(Y(t) = Level + Trend + Seasonality + Noise\)
  • Multiplicative Model: \(Y(t) = Level * Trend * Seasonality * Noise\)

The additive model is linear where changes over time are consistently made by the same amount. The multiplicative model is nonlinear, where changes over time are proportional to the level of the time series. Since we have some zero values in our data we can't use the multiplicative model, but we will use the additive model.

So for each brand we consider two different time series:

  • Phones time series: it is the time series of the number of phones released by the brand in a given year
  • Videos time series: it is the time series of the number of videos released about the brand in a given year.

For both of them we used the additive model, and we considered only the Seasonality component because we consider it as the most informative one; notice that we plot the seasonality only for one year since by definition (as said before) it is periodic (in our case yearly). Now we can analyze the plot: we can see that indeed Apple is the only brand of those in our analysis that has the video's seasonality (i.e. the seasonality of released Apple videos) that mirrors almost perfectly the seasonality of phones (i.e the seasonality of released Apple phones). This supports our thesis: Apple is the only brand that has a strong relationship between the release of new phones and the release of new videos, meaning that Apple can greatly benefit from Youtube's "free marketing". In other words when an Apple phone is released, it immediately receives a lot of media coverage from youtube videos.
